Maven Community Guidelines

At Maven, we believe that an honest community of women and healthcare practitioners is a powerful antidote to online misinformation and confusion.

Our conversations allow women to be anonymous because we believe it makes it easier to speak freely about private health questions—but we share the identity of our Maven Practitioners so you can get to know them and their backgrounds.

Maven is not a pre-moderated forum, so we ask that everyone respect the following community guidelines:

YES:

  • Be open-minded and supportive

  • Respect the thoughts and questions of the community

  • Report bad behavior so we can take the appropriate actions

  • Say whatever you want to make this an honest place where other members of the community feel comfortable sharing

  • Try to use good grammar and spelling—it makes it easier for everyone to read

NO:

  • Don’t treat any of this as medical advice—and always call 911 for urgent medical issues

  • Don’t post someone’s personal information

  • Don’t post anything illegal

  • Don’t harass, troll, or otherwise purposefully try and make someone else feel miserable

  • Don’t post threats—we will delete them and reserve the right to flag to authorities if there is an issue

  • Don’t post terrible things like racism or hate speech

  • Don’t post anything that’s spammy, commercial, or would be considered advertising

  • The Maven community is about supporting each other, and we won’t tolerate anyone that tries to stop our forum from being a welcoming place


Maven can terminate your access at any time for abusing these guidelines. Please remember that Maven's Terms of Use and Privacy Policy apply.
